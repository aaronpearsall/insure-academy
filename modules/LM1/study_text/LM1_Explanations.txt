Question 1
A commercial customer applying for office insurance has been burgled a number of times recently. In accordance with the principle of good faith, when must this be disclosed to the insurer?

A. During the proposal stage.
B. Only if a specific question is asked.
C. Immediately after the quotation is received.
D. Immediately after the premium is paid.

Answer: A
Explanation: Under the duty of fair presentation (and the principle of good faith), material facts that the insurer would want to know must be disclosed during the proposal stage, before the contract is concluded. A history of burglaries is material to the risk and must be disclosed at that time.

Question 2
Which insurance principle allows an insurer, once it has indemnified the insured, to take over the insured ’s right to recover payment from a third party responsible for a loss?

A. Contribution.
B. Proximate cause.
C. Subrogation.
D. Good faith.

Answer: C
Explanation: Subrogation is the principle that allows an insurer, once it has indemnified the insured, to take over the insured's right to recover payment from a third party responsible for the loss. The insurer's right to subrogate depends on the insured having rights against that party (for example, a negligent motorist). In some situations insurers are barred from exercising subrogation rights, or agree not to do so.

Question 54
A building valued at £250 ,000 is jointly owned by Karen and Terry , who each individually arrange insurance on it. Karen insures the building for £100 ,000 , whilst Terry insures it for £150 ,000. If a valid claim of £55 ,000 occurs how much is each insurer liable to pay?

A. Karen ’s insurer is liable for £22 ,000 and Terry ’s for £33 ,000.
B. Karen ’s insurer is liable for £27 ,500 and Terry ’s for £27 ,500.
C. Karen ’s insurer is liable for £33 ,000 and Terry ’s for £22 ,000.
D. Karen ’s insurer is liable for £55 ,000 and Terry ’s for £55 ,000.

Answer: A
Explanation: Under the sum insured method, each insurer pays in proportion to their share of the total sum insured. Total sum insured is £100,000 + £150,000 = £250,000. Karen's insurer pays (100,000 ÷ 250,000) × £55,000 = £22,000; Terry's pays (150,000 ÷ 250,000) × £55,000 = £33,000.
