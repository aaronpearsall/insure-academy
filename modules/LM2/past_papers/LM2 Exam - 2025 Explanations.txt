Question 6
An insurer has a maximum line of $100,000 on any one risk and decides to buy a four-line surplus reinsurance. If a risk has a sum insured of $400,000, what percentage of the risk is ceded to reinsurers?

A. 20%
B. 25%
C. 75%
D. 100%

Answer: C
Explanation: Insurer retains $100,000 (its max line) of $400,000 risk. Four-line surplus means reinsurers take 4 × $100,000 = $300,000. Ceded = $300,000 ÷ $400,000 = 75%.
